faith

Definition:

Faith is trust or confidence in someone or something.

  • Faith is the practice of believing in, trusting in, or relying on someone or something.
  • Faith in someone is believing that he always says what is true and he always does what he says he will do.
  • When a person believes in God and trusts Jesus to save him, that person has faith.

If someone truly has faith in God, that person will act on that faith by obeying God.

[See also: believe, faithful]

Examples from the Bible stories:

  • [05-06] When Isaac was a young man, God tested Abraham's faith by saying, “Take Isaac, your only son, and kill him as a sacrifice to me.”
  • [31-07] Then he (Jesus) said to Peter, “You man of little faith, why did you doubt?”
  • [32-16] Jesus said to her, “Your faith has healed you. Go in peace.”
  • [38-09] Then Jesus said to Peter, “Satan wants to have all of you, but I have prayed for you, Peter, that your faith will not fail.
