trust

(trust, to trust)

Definition:

To trust someone or something is to believe that they are reliable, honest, true, and dependable.

  • Trust is closely related to faith. We trust someone because we have faith that they will do what they promised to do.
  • Trusting in Jesus means believing that he is God, believing that his sacrificial death on the cross paid for our sins, and depending on him to save us.
  • Often people foolishly trust in themselves, other people, or things to meet their needs, even though these cannot bring them the good that they desire.

[See also: faith]

Examples from the Bible stories:

  • [12-12] When the Israelites saw that the Egyptians were dead, they trusted in God and believed that Moses was a prophet of God.
  • [14-15] Joshua was a good leader because he trusted and obeyed God.
  • [17-02] David was a humble and righteous man who trusted and obeyed God.
  • [34-06] Then Jesus told a story about people who trusted in their own good deeds and despised other people.
